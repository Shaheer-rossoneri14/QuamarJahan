Prof. Quamar Jahan: A Luminary in Urdu Literature


Early Life and Education
Prof. Quamar Jahan, born on May 15, 1948, in the serene village of Buzurgdwar in Darbhanga District, Bihar, is a distinguished figure in the world of Urdu literature. Raised by her parents, Late Syed Ata-ul-Haque and Late Biwi Akhtar Jahan, she completed her early schooling in Barh and attended Bankipore Girls High School in Patna. Her academic journey continued at Magadh Mahila College in Patna, where she pursued a B.A. in Urdu Honours. Driven by her passion for the Urdu language, she went on to complete her M.A. in Urdu from Patna University and later earned her Ph.D. from Ranchi University.
Academic and Professional Achievements
Prof. Jahan's professional career commenced on December 9, 1970, as a lecturer in the Department of Urdu at S.M. College, Bhagalpur. Her dedication and expertise soon led her to the PG Department of Urdu at Tilkamanjhi Bhagalpur University. Over the years, she held prominent positions, including serving two terms as the Dean of Humanities and Language and being a member of the Senate at Bhagalpur University.
Literary Contributions
Prof. Jahan's literary career is marked by a prolific output and a profound impact on modern Urdu literature. Her first story, "Junoon-e-Wafa," published in 1964, was just the beginning of a journey that saw her works featured in leading Urdu magazines such as Beeswi Sadi, Aajkal, Aiwan-e-Urdu, and Shayar Bombay. She also became a familiar voice on various radio programs since the 1970s. Among her notable works are the short story collections "Charahgar" (1983) and "Ajnabi Chehre" (1991, with a second edition in 1995), and her critical writings "Meyar" (1988) and "Tanisi Tanqeed Mumtaz Shirin Se Ahd-e-Hazir Tak" (2014). She has also contributed to the academic field with works like "Kalam Abdullah Hafiz Mushkipuri" (2008) and "Professor Alimullah Hali Nukush-o-Afkaar" (2014). Her upcoming collection, "Yaadnagar," is eagerly anticipated by her readers.
Engagements and Contributions
Prof. Jahan has been an active participant in numerous national and international seminars, sharing her insights and expertise with the Urdu literary fraternity. Her contributions as a subject matter expert have been recognized by various universities, and she has served on multiple selection committees, enhancing the academic and literary standards in her field. Her involvement with various societies, including serving as a member of the NCPUL Delhi for two terms and the Executive Committee of the Urdu Academy in Patna, showcases her dedication to the promotion of Urdu literature. As the Secretary of Anjuman Mata-e-Kalam, she has been instrumental in fostering a community of contemporary women writers.
Awards and Recognitions
Throughout her career, Prof. Jahan has been honored with prestigious awards, including the National Fellowship Award by the UGC Delhi and the Imtiaz-e-meer by the Fakhruddin Ali Ahmad Memorial Committee in Lucknow in 1997.
Personal Life
Prof. Quamar Jahan is married to M.B. Hasan, a Divisional Engineer, and they have two children, Hasan Perwez and Seema Hasan. Beyond her academic and literary pursuits, she enjoys writing stories and critiques, with a particular interest in modern literature. Prof. Quamar Jahan's remarkable journey in the realm of Urdu literature is a testament to her dedication, talent, and passion for the literary arts. Her extensive body of work continues to inspire and influence readers and scholars alike.
